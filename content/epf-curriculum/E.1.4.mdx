# Module E.1.4: Incentives & Profits

## Standard: EPF.E.1.4

**Objective:** Analyze the role of incentives and profits in a market economy.

---

## Lesson Overview
- Define incentives and profits
- Discuss how incentives drive behavior
- Explore examples in business and government

## Teacher Notes
- Use real-world business case studies
- Facilitate discussion on ethical incentives
- Connect to student experiences (rewards, grades)

## Activities
- Incentive design challenge
- Debate: Are all incentives good?
- Analyze business profit motives

## Assessment
- Short essay: Incentives in my life
- Group discussion participation

## Questions

1. **Multiple Choice:** What is an incentive?
   - a) A punishment for bad behavior
   - b) A reward or penalty that motivates action
   - c) A type of government policy
   - d) A business expense

2. **Short Answer:** Give an example of a positive incentive in school.

3. **Multiple Choice:** Which of the following is a profit incentive for businesses?
   - a) Lowering prices to attract customers
   - b) Ignoring customer needs
   - c) Reducing product quality
   - d) Avoiding innovation

4. **Short Answer:** Explain how incentives can sometimes lead to unintended consequences.

5. **Multiple Choice:** Which sector is most likely to use profit incentives?
   - a) Government
   - b) Nonprofit
   - c) Private business
   - d) Public schools

6. **Short Answer:** Describe a situation where a government incentive changed people's behavior.

7. **Multiple Choice:** Which of the following is NOT an example of an incentive?
   - a) Tax break
   - b) Bonus pay
   - c) Law of supply
   - d) Award certificate

8. **Short Answer:** Why are incentives important in a market economy?
